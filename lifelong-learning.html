Lifelong Learning

Continuously expanding my knowledge and skills has been the cornerstone of my journey. My academic and research experiences have shaped my understanding of science and fueled my passion for discovery.

Academic Excellence

My academic journey began at UCI where I earned my Bachelor's in Biological Sciences—making the Dean's List for five consecutive quarters. This rigorous curriculum laid the foundation for my passion in research and discovery.

Research and Innovation

I gained invaluable laboratory experience across two research environments:

Dr. Kleinman's Group: I contributed to in vivo studies examining the effects of vape, cigarette, and hookah exposure on the development of atherosclerosis. My work focused on assessing the super oxidative stress released by lung macrophages using assays such as DTT. I learned advanced techniques like immunohistochemistry and RNA sequencing, culminating in a publication and a poster presentation.

Dr. Fowler's Neurobiology Lab: I served as a laboratory technician where I assisted with in vivo Skinner box experiments to study drug addiction behaviors and supported surgical procedures by establishing intravenous injection pathways and extracting CSF fluid.
